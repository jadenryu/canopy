## 💻 General advice
 
Hackathons are hard. Shipping something compelling within 2 days is a challenge, even with AI. Here are some tips to maximize your chances of success:
 
- Pick the right team size. Solo can leave a lot of work on your plate, but 5 people you’ve never worked with can be hard to coordinate.
- You can solo hack, but a hackathon is also an ideal place to find project collaborators and even cofounders. Take advantage of that. Meet people, learn what they’re building, connect with each other.
- Integrate project components early. I’ve seen a lot of teams split the work into backend/frontend/etc, develop independently, then try to put it all together in the last hour. This rarely works. Instead, you can split the work, but make sure you’re checking code in early and often so compatibility doesn’t diverge too much.
- Use the sponsor tools, and take advantage of their onsite engineering help. No one is better with their tools than they are, and they’re here to help! Don’t hesitate to ask. Find them via the table signs.
- Git commit early and often. Not only does this give you checkpoints to revert to when AI fucks something up, it also provides a clear signal to the judges that this is a project you built this weekend, not before.
- Make a brief post on social media immediately after the hackathon. You’ll be tired, but it’s key to get this live before you lose momentum. This post serves as a public signal that you can ship working code and that you have a unique perspective in AI, even if it’s a janky prototype. They can also go viral!
 
## 📩 Submissions
 
All hackathon submissions will go on Cerebral Valley platform. The link will be sent out Sunday morning. 
 
Every submission requires a few things. First, you need to link a public GitHub repository so judges can inspect your code. Second, make sure you list every team member. Third, include a description of every sponsor tool you used and every agent protocol you used. Finally, a demo video is optional, but we highly recommend either a video or some explanatory pictures to make it easier for judges to remember your project when reviewing online. 
 
In both the preliminary and final rounds, participants will have exactly 3 minutes (strictly enforced) to demonstrate what they built. This should have a heavy emphasis on the demo itself and include no more than one or two slides. Their judges will have a chance to ask one or two questions at the end of your presentation. 
 
Make sure you have Zoom installed on your computer! Or that you can access the Zoom web sharing link, https://share.zoom.us
 
### Make sure your submission includes the following:
 
- A unique team name
- Names of all team members (make sure your CV account has emails and socials so we can contact you!)
- A quick screen recording demo of what you built (less than 2 mins!)
- Your X and/or LinkedIn handles, so we can tag you if we feature your project on socials
- A thorough description including
    - A 2-3 sentence summary of what you built
    - What your project does / what it’s useful for
    - How it’s built. Explanation of which RL environments, orchestration protocols (like A2A, MCP), and agent application frameworks you used.
    - A list of every sponsor tool/protocol you used and how you used them. (This is critical for both sponsor prizes and grand prizes)
 
### 🧑‍⚖️ Evaluation criteria
 
Judging is going to happen in two stages. First, you will be assigned a presentation room based on the above map (more detail later on), then you will present in front of a panel of judges in a room for 3 minutes. Runner ups will present in front of judges and everyone in the final round of presentations. 
 
Here are the judging criteria:
 
- **Creativity.** How unique is the project? Is either the problem or the approach new?
- **Harness sophistication.** Does the project meaningfully show a team of agents working well together?
- **Utility.** How useful is this project? Does it solve a real problem?
- **Technical execution.** Does it work? Did they make reasonable architecture decisions?
- **Sponsor usage.** Did they meaningfully use one or more sponsor tools?
 
## Judging Mechanics
 
Hackers, we will start our initial round of judging at 1:30pm. 
 
We will call out your teams name and will announce which room you will be presenting at. The rooms are in the back of the venue along the wall. You will have 3 minute to present with optional 2 minutes for questions. 
 
The criteria you will be evaluated on are: 
 
**Impact Potential/ Utility -** Does the project address a significant real-world issue or pain point?
**Technical Demo -** How is the demo? Is the project implemented technically well? Is it well-engineered and working?
**Creativity** - How creative is the project? Have you seen this before?
**Presentation & Pitch** - How well was the project presented and demonstrated?
**Multi-agent harness sophistication** - Does the project meaningfully show a complex, effective environment?
 
Second round in front of live audience - we’ll gather everyone, and will announce the 8 finalists one by one. You must be present to be able to present otherwise we’ll go to the next project on the list. 
 
Prizes Ceremony - We will announce sponsor prizes first and then we will announce the three winners for the grand prizes!
